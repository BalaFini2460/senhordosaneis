Sauron was originally a Maia spirit, created by the Ainur before the beginning of the world. He was a servant of Aulë, the smith of the Valar, and possessed great skill in craftsmanship and construction. However, Sauron was corrupted by Melkor (Morgoth), becoming his most loyal and powerful lieutenant.

The One Ring

In the Second Age, Sauron, under the guise of "Annatar," the Lord of Gifts, taught the elves of Eregion to forge the Rings of Power. Secretly, he created the One Ring in Mount Doom, in Mordor, to control all the other rings. The Ring was the focus of his strength and connected him to the creatures who used the other rings. When the elves discovered his betrayal, they hid the Three Elven Rings, beginning a war between Sauron and the Free Peoples. After Sauron was defeated by the Last Alliance, the Ring was lost and remained hidden until it was found by Gollum and, later, by Frodo.

Sauron slowly regained his strength and manifested as the "Necromancer" in Dol Guldur, in Mirkwood. He later returned to Mordor, rebuilding Barad-dûr and gathering his armies. During the War of the Ring, he attempted to recover the lost artifact, but Frodo Baggins destroyed the Ring in the Cracks of Doom. This resulted in the complete destruction of Sauron's power, reducing him to a powerless spirit, incapable of manifesting again.
