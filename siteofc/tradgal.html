Origin

Galadriel is an elf of the high lineage of the Noldor and the Teleri, daughter of Finarfin and granddaughter of Finwë, the first king of the Noldor. Endowed with great beauty and wisdom, she was one of the leaders of the exile of the Noldor to Middle-earth after the rebellion against the Valar. During the First Age, she lived in Doriath, where she learned wisdom from Melian, the Maia. Although not directly involved in Fëanor's rebellion, she was banned from Valinor for crossing the sea.

Galadriel settled in various regions of Middle-earth, eventually becoming the Lady of Lothlórien alongside her husband, Celeborn. During this time, she received one of the Three Elven Rings, Nenya, the Ring of Water, which she used to protect and preserve the beauty of Lothlórien. When Sauron attempted to dominate Middle-earth with the One Ring, Galadriel resisted his power and helped organize the resistance against him.

Third Age and the War of the Ring

In the Third Age, Galadriel played a crucial role in the War of the Ring. She welcomed the Fellowship of the Ring in Lothlórien, offering advice and gifts that helped in the mission to destroy the One Ring. She was also tempted by the power of the Ring but resisted, stating that she would "diminish and pass into the West" rather than succumb to corruption.
