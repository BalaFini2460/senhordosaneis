Origins and History

Hobbits do not have a well-documented origin in Tolkien's universe, but it is known that they emerged at some point during the Third Age, likely descending from humans. They are a reserved race, rarely involving themselves in the larger world's events, and prefer to live in peace.

-The first mention of hobbits occurs when they migrated to the Shire in the year 1601 of the Third Age.

-Despite being small in number and influence, hobbits like Bilbo and Frodo played decisive roles in the War of the Ring.

Characteristics of Hobbits

1- Stature and Appearance:

- Hobbits are about 1 meter tall.

- They have hairy and sturdy feet, which makes wearing shoes unnecessary.

2- Personality and Culture:

- They are peaceful, hospitable, and love to eat (they eat at least six meals a day if possible).

- They value simplicity, comfort, and family life.

- Hobbits avoid conflicts but show unexpected courage when faced with challenges.

3- Notable Abilities:

- They are extremely stealthy, especially in natural environments.

- They possess great resilience and determination, as seen in Frodo and Sam.

Main Hobbit Group

Harfoots:

- The most numerous lineage.

- They have darker skin and smaller feet.

- They prefer hilly areas and are the simplest of the hobbits.
