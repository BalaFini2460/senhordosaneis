Legolas is a woodland elf, son of Thranduil, king of the Mirkwood (formerly Greenwood), located in northern Middle-earth. He is a Sindar elf but lives among the Silvan elves, reflecting a strong connection with nature.

Participation in the Fellowship of the Ring

Legolas was chosen as the representative of the elves in the Fellowship of the Ring during the Council of Elrond. He accompanied Frodo and the other members on the mission to destroy the One Ring, demonstrating loyalty, courage, and exceptional archery skills.

During the journey, Legolas developed a special friendship with Gimli, a dwarf, overcoming the traditional rivalries between their races. His relationship with Aragorn was also one of respect and camaraderie, recognizing the future king as a worthy leader.

War of the Ring

Legolas participated in important battles, such as the defense of Helm's Deep and the battle at the Ports of Cair Andros. His courage and skills were crucial to the Fellowship's success on several occasions.

After the destruction of the One Ring and Aragorn's coronation as king, Legolas remained in Middle-earth for some time. Eventually, he built a boat and sailed to the Undying Lands (Valinor) along with Gimli, fulfilling his promise of eternal friendship.
