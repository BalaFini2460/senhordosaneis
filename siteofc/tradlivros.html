The Lord of the Rings Books

The Fellowship of the Ring

Published in 1954, this is the first book of the trilogy. It tells the story of Frodo Baggins, a hobbit from the Shire, who inherits the One Ring from his uncle Bilbo and embarks on a perilous journey to destroy it. Along the way, Frodo forms the Fellowship of the Ring, composed of hobbits, an elf, a dwarf, a wizard, and men, to assist him in his quest.

The Two Towers

Also published in 1954, the second book of the trilogy narrates the separation of the Fellowship and the challenges each group faces. This volume explores themes of friendship, sacrifice, and corruption.

The Return of the King

Published in 1955, the final volume of the trilogy culminates in the ultimate battle against Sauron. Aragorn claims his place as the king of Gondor, and Frodo and Sam face the final challenges to destroy the One Ring at Mount Doom. This book concludes the story with heroism, sacrifice, and a sense of farewell.
